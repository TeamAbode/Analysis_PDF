How we got these numbers. We simulated thousands of randomly-drawn 12-juror panels from this respondent pool and asked: in how many of them would jurors reach the legal threshold to find for the plaintiff, and at what dollar amount? Each simulated juror's award is weighted by how much fault they personally assigned to the defendant, but only if that juror said "yes" to liability. Extreme outlier awards from one or two respondents are dampened so they don't distort the projection. The scenarios above describe the range a mediator can realistically expect — a typical day, a great day for plaintiff, and a great day for defense.
